Engineers at MindLink do not always stop to celebrate successes, so over the years I made a point of holding onto a few keepsakes that mattered to me. I recently brought them together into this small framed piece of memorabilia, which reflects six years of growth, collaboration, and moments I am proud to remember.

![A framed display of MindLink keepsakes](mindlink_shadowbox.jpg)

As a small side project, I collected a few keepsakes from that time and turned them into this framed piece: a MindLink card, a DSEI UK coin, and the medal from winning the MindLink Hackathon. One of the tokens is blurred in the photo because of the confidential nature of the clients we support, which feels like a fairly honest reflection of the kind of work we do. On the surface it is just something decorative for a shelf, but for me it represents years of steady progress, trust, and shared effort.

I like projects like this because they give physical form to work that is usually invisible. Software engineering often lives in documents, pull requests, and production systems; this is a reminder that the best parts of the job are also personal: growth, collaboration, and pride in building something worthwhile.
